Rice Kheer Recipe

The Indian sweet dish (dessert) Kheer is usually made with cereals, lentils, or their flours, and sometimes even fruits and veggies. It resembles a pudding and is traditionally made with milk, sugar, and dry fruits.

The flavoring includes spices like saffron, cardamom, and nutmeg, as well as ingredients like rose water and kewra water. Modern versions may use vanilla or other flavors.

Indian cuisine has many variations of Kheer. Rice Kheer is the Indian variant of rice pudding, made with basmati rice, whole milk, sugar, ground spices, and nuts.

Regional versions like Bengali Payesh use nolen gur (date palm jaggery) instead of sugar, giving a light brown or beige color. Along with Seviyan Kheer (vermicelli pudding) and Phirni (thick rice pudding), Rice Kheer is one of the most popular Kheer variations in North Indian cuisine.
